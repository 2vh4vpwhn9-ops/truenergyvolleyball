Module 2 — Float Serve

Coach's Golden Rule

No Spin = True Float Serve

A real float serve must travel through the air with little or no spin. If the ball is spinning after contact, it is not a true float serve.

This is the main progress indicator for this module: the ball should float, move unpredictably, and rotate as little as possible.

Technique

Starting Position & Crown Position

Start in a balanced position with one foot in front. The exact position of the arms and legs can change depending on whether the player is right-handed or left-handed.

The non-hitting hand holds the ball in front of the body with the arm extended. The hitting hand starts behind the head in the “crown position” — like the player is holding a crown behind the head.

The hitting hand should be flat like a board. The fingers stay together and are pressed firmly against each other.

At contact, the hitting hand strikes the ball cleanly and then stops sharply. The arm should not continue forward like an attacking swing.

Success Indicator

How You Know It Works

  • The ball flies with little or no spin.
  • The player stays balanced from start to finish.
  • The body does not lean left or right.
  • The body does not fall forward or backward.
  • The hitting hand stops immediately after contact.
  • The ball travels with a floating effect instead of regular rotation.
Common Mistakes

What To Avoid

  • The ball spins after contact.
  • The fingers are open or relaxed.
  • The hand is not flat like a board.
  • The arm continues forward like an attack swing.
  • The player leans to the left or right.
  • The player leans forward or backward during contact.
  • The ball is held too close to the body.
Coach's Key Points
  • One foot starts in front for balance.
  • The ball is held in front with the non-hitting arm extended.
  • The hitting hand starts behind the head in the crown position.
  • The hand must be flat like a board.
  • Fingers stay together and firm.
  • After contact, the hitting arm stops sharply.
  • Do not lean forward, backward, left, or right.
Skill Check
  • Can you serve the ball with little or no spin?
  • Can you keep your body balanced after contact?
  • Can you stop the hitting hand sharply after contact?
  • Can you keep your fingers together and firm?
  • Can you avoid leaning forward, backward, left, or right?
